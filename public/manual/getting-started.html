Getting Started

Everything you need to survive your first year in Exiled.

Your First Priority: Survive Winter

You start in early spring with 20 adults, 2 children, and a modest stockpile. Winter will arrive in about 10 real-time minutes at 1× speed. When it hits, temperatures plunge below freezing. If your citizens don't have firewood, coats, houses, and food, they will die.

Step-by-Step: First 5 Minutes

  1. Place a Stockpile — It's free and builds almost instantly. This gives your resources a home and prevents overflow.
  2. Build a Storage Barn — Barns reduce food spoilage by 80%. Without one, your starting food will rot.
  3. Build a Gathering Hut near forest — Assign 4 gatherers. This is your primary food source until farms produce.
  4. Build a Hunting Cabin — Assign 3 hunters. Provides venison (food) and leather (for coats).
  5. Build 4–5 Wooden Houses — Each houses 5 residents. You need at least 5 houses for 22 people.
  6. Build a Wood Cutter — Assign 1 worker. Converts logs into firewood. Houses burn firewood to stay warm.
  7. Build a Forester Lodge — Assign 2–4 foresters. Without foresters, you'll run out of trees (and logs) permanently.

Next Priorities

  1. Fishing Dock (if near water) — Fish are available year-round, making docks your most reliable food source.
  2. Blacksmith — Tools wear out. Workers without tools produce 50% less. Start forging before your starting tools break.
  3. Tailor — Coats wear out too. Citizens without coats lose warmth 2× faster.
  4. Herbalist — Gathers herbs that automatically heal sick citizens. Disease can spread fast without one.
  5. Crop Field — Plant in spring, harvest in autumn. Wheat also produces hay for livestock.

Things That Will Kill Your Village

Winter Without Firewood

Houses go cold, citizens freeze, health drops, people die. Always keep firewood production ahead of consumption. Build a Wood Cutter early.

Food Spoilage

Food rots over time. Without a Storage Barn, your stockpile can decay into nothing. Build a barn before winter.

Tool Shortage

When tools break and you have no iron or Blacksmith, all tool-dependent workers (farmers, hunters, fishermen, foresters) produce at half rate.

Uncontrolled Growth

More people means more mouths to feed. If births outpace food production, you spiral into starvation. Balance growth with resources.

Tips from Experienced Players

  • Diverse food = happy citizens. Eating 3+ food types gives a happiness boost. Only eating one type causes unhappiness.
  • Roads matter. Dirt Roads double movement speed. Connect your buildings early.
  • Don't overbuild. Every construction site pulls laborers away from other jobs. Build what you need, when you need it.
  • Watch the seasons. Plant crops in spring, stockpile in autumn, and survive winter. Check the Seasons page for details.
  • Upgrade buildings once you're stable. Stone Barns preserve food 90% better, Sawmills produce 50% more firewood.
  • Build a Town Hall when you can afford it. Festivals provide powerful seasonal bonuses.
